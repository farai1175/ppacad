Definitions and Scientific Principles

Boyle's Law:

States that the volume (V) of a fixed amount of gas is inversely proportional to its pressure (P) at constant temperature.

Inverse Proportionality:

A relationship where one variable decreases proportionally as the other increases.

Proportionality Constant (k):

A fixed number used to change the proportional relationship into an equation. For Boyle's Law, pV = k.

Pressure (P):

The pressure in a gas is caused by the gas molecules colliding with the walls of a container.

Fixed Amount of Gas:

Boyle's Law requires that the amount (mass or number of moles, n) of the gas remains constant.

Constant Temperature:

If the temperature is not kept constant, the proportionality is broken, as cooling or heating generally causes the gas to contract or expand, or the pressure to decrease or increase.

Introduction

Boyle's Law, named after Robert Boyle, is one of the foundational gas laws that describe the behaviour of gases. This law focuses on the quantifiable relationship between the volume and the pressure of a gas when the temperature and amount of gas are held constant. Understanding this relationship is crucial for explaining everyday phenomena, such as how breathing works.

Main Body: Investigation and Description of Boyle's Law

Boyle's Law states that the volume of a fixed amount of gas is inversely proportional to its pressure at constant temperature.

The relationship between volume (V) and pressure (P) is described as inversely proportional.

  • In symbols, this relationship is written as: V ∝ 1/P.
  • When this is written as an equation using a proportionality constant (k), it becomes pV = k.
  • This relationship implies that if the pressure on an enclosed gas doubles, its volume will halve (at constant temperature). If the pressure increases three times, its volume will be divided by three.
V ∝ 1/P
pV = k

In an experiment designed to verify Boyle's law (such as using a syringe attached to a pressure gauge), it is essential to identify and control variables.

The variables in a typical Boyle's Law investigation are:

  • Independent Variable: Volume (V). The independent variable is the quantity that the investigator changes or allows to change. In the experiment described in the sources, the volume is intentionally changed by pressing the syringe plunger down. Volume is typically plotted on the x-axis.
  • Dependent Variable: Pressure (P). The dependent variable is the quantity that the independent variable causes to change. Pressure is measured as a result of the volume change. Pressure is typically plotted on the y-axis.
  • Controlled Variables: Temperature and the amount of gas (fixed mass or number of moles). These variables must be kept constant to ensure a fair test. Maintaining constant temperature is particularly important, as increasing the temperature would break the proportionality.

Plotting the experimental results visually helps to describe the mathematical relationship between the variables.

1. Graph of Volume versus Pressure (V vs P):

  • Plotting Volume (independent variable, x-axis) against Pressure (dependent variable, y-axis) produces a curved graph known as a hyperbola.
  • The hyperbolic shape visually represents the inverse relationship: as pressure increases, volume decreases.

2. Graph of Volume versus Inverse Pressure (V vs 1/P):

  • To be certain that a curved graph is a hyperbola and to verify the inverse proportionality, one must draw a graph where the volume (V) is plotted against the inverse of the pressure (1/P).
  • Plotting V versus 1/P yields a straight line.
  • If this straight line passes through the origin, it confirms that volume is inversely proportional to pressure. The product pV is a constant value at a specific temperature.

Conclusion

Boyle's Law is fundamental to gas dynamics, defining the inverse relationship between the volume and pressure of a fixed mass of gas at constant temperature, summarized mathematically as pV = k. This relationship is confirmed experimentally by observing the variables in the Boyle's Law investigation. Crucially, while a graph of V vs P is a hyperbola, plotting V vs 1/P produces a straight line through the origin, offering definitive visual proof of this inverse proportionality.
